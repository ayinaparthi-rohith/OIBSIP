# Introduction

As organizations increasingly rely on digital technologies and internet-connected systems, network security has become a critical aspect of protecting information and business operations. Cyber attackers continuously attempt to exploit vulnerabilities in networks to gain unauthorized access, steal sensitive information, disrupt services, or compromise systems.

Understanding common network security threats is an important responsibility for cybersecurity professionals. By studying how these attacks operate and the risks they create, organizations can implement effective security controls and reduce the likelihood of successful attacks.

This task focuses on four major network security threats:

* Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks
* Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) Attacks
* Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks
* Spoofing Attacks


# Importance of Network Security

Network security helps protect systems, applications, and data from unauthorized access, misuse, and cyberattacks.

The primary goals of network security are:

### Confidentiality

Ensuring that sensitive information is accessible only to authorized users.

### Integrity

Protecting information from unauthorized modification or tampering.

### Availability

Ensuring that systems, applications, and services remain available to legitimate users when needed.

A failure to maintain these principles can lead to financial losses, operational disruption, and damage to an organization's reputation.


# Network Security Threats Covered

## 1. Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks

A Denial of Service attack is designed to make a system, server, or network unavailable to legitimate users by overwhelming it with excessive requests or traffic.

### How It Works

1. The attacker selects a target system.
2. Large amounts of traffic are generated.
3. System resources become exhausted.
4. Legitimate users are unable to access the service.

### Impact

* Service downtime
* Business disruption
* Reduced productivity
* Financial losses

### Prevention

* Firewalls
* Rate limiting
* Traffic filtering
* Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS)

---

## 2. Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) Attacks

A DDoS attack is similar to a DoS attack but involves multiple compromised devices working together to attack a target system.

### How It Works

1. Multiple devices are infected with malware.
2. The infected devices form a botnet.
3. The attacker controls the botnet remotely.
4. Massive amounts of traffic are sent to the target simultaneously.

### Impact

* Large-scale service outages
* Network congestion
* Resource exhaustion
* Revenue loss

### Real-World Example

The Mirai Botnet attack used thousands of compromised IoT devices to launch large-scale DDoS attacks against major online services.

### Prevention

* DDoS protection services
* Load balancing
* Content Delivery Networks (CDN)
* Network monitoring

---

## 3. Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks

A Man-in-the-Middle attack occurs when an attacker secretly intercepts communication between two parties without their knowledge.

### How It Works

1. Two users establish communication.
2. The attacker positions themselves between the communication channel.
3. Data is intercepted and monitored.
4. Information may be modified or stolen.

### Risks

* Credential theft
* Session hijacking
* Financial fraud
* Data leakage

### Prevention

* HTTPS encryption
* Virtual Private Networks (VPN)
* Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
* Secure wireless networks

---

## 4. Spoofing Attacks

Spoofing is a technique where an attacker impersonates a trusted source to deceive users or systems.

### Types of Spoofing

#### IP Spoofing

Manipulating IP addresses to appear as another system.

#### Email Spoofing

Forging email sender addresses to trick recipients.

#### ARP Spoofing

Redirecting network traffic by manipulating ARP tables.

#### DNS Spoofing

Providing false DNS information to redirect users to malicious websites.

### Impact

* Identity impersonation
* Unauthorized access
* Malware distribution
* Credential theft

### Prevention

* Email authentication protocols
* Network monitoring
* Secure DNS configurations
* Access control mechanisms
